# Elevator pitch

Robotics is bleeding money on 3D cameras. Zero Depth uses standard RGB lenses and voxel carving to synthesize 3D grasp coordinates, streaming them to the robot for a fraction of the cost.

Industrial robotics is currently bleeding money on expensive depth-cameras and lengthy calibration processes just to navigate cluttered factory bins. Our Zero Depth project stops the bleeding by unlocking grip stability using standard, highly efficient RGB cameras. Our software leverages a custom multi-view fusion layer and shape-from-silhouette voxel carving to instantly synthesize a precise 3D model directly from 2D photos. It then ranks parallel-jaw grasp candidates and streams the exact coordinates directly to hardware, like UR10e. We’ve completely decoupled the vision engine from the robotic arm, delivering a validated, real-time control loop that conquers bin chaos for a fraction of the traditional cost.
